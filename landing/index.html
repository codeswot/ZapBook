ZapBook is a mobile reading application where finishing a book earns real Bitcoin. Readers read, pass comprehension milestones, and get zapped sats directly from their reading circle. No platform tokens. Real money.

The Problem How it works
The Problem

We tell kids reading matters — then reward them with gold stars.

Hollow Rewards

Stickers, pizza coupons, and screen time. Artificial incentives fade the moment the chart fills up.

No Real Proof

Reading logs are pure guesswork. Parents can't easily distinguish pages flipped from pages actually comprehended.

Solitary Journey

Reading is solitary. Kids miss shared goals, social streaks, and the encouragement of a community cheering them on.

The Solution

Put real stakes behind every page.

ZapBook shifts incentives from hollow badges to tangible savings. Every milestone passed is backed by proof, paid out instantly in Bitcoin.

Workflow

Three steps, one stream.

01

Read the Book

A distraction-free, beautifully formatted reading screen. Our smart local reading engine tracks active page dwell time, pacing, and micro-interactions to verify genuine reader engagement.

02

Pass the Milestone

When readers reach a checkpoint, a comprehension quiz is dynamically generated to prove comprehension. (Coming soon: quiz generation runs entirely on-device using a local AI model for offline privacy.)

03

Get Zapped Sats

Correct answers automatically trigger instant microtransactions (zaps) sent straight to their wallet address via Lightning.

The Stack

Built on open-source, peer-to-peer protocols.

Lightning

Instant, low-fee value transfers that make paying micro-amounts (sats) viable for daily reading gains.

Nostr

Decentralized key-pair identities and secure messaging relays. Completely free of corporate walled gardens.

Marmot Protocol

Decentralized end-to-end encrypted (E2EE) group messaging built on MLS (Messaging Layer Security) using Nostr for identity and transport.

On-Device AI (Gemma)

Utilizes an offline Gemma model running locally on the device to generate milestone comprehension checks, preserving privacy and eliminating server dependencies.
